Hi! I am Marie (she/her). I am a second-year PhD student at NIMH, USA and the University of Lisbon, Portugal. I am very lucky to be supervised by Dr Daniel Pine at the NIH and Dr Diana Prata at the University of Lisbon. My work is also mentored by Prof Argyris Stringaris at the UCL and Dr Dylan Nielson at the NIMH Machine Learning Team. My main research interest is affective neuroscience. In my daily work, I use Python and High-performance computing.

Before joining NIMH, I spent a few years doing social neurocience research at DPrataLab. In particular, I looked at the effect of oxytocin on human resting-state EEG (2022 paper in Cerebral Cortex).
Before that, I obtained an MSc in Neurobiology from the University of Porto and an MSc in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Edinburgh, where I analysed human eye-tracking data.

I am neurodiverse, and I care a lot about inclusivity, health and work-life balance in academia.

Outside of research, I enjoy cooking and eating my way through the world’s map (can’t ever say no to Goan fish curry!), spending time in nature and winter sports. I also have an unhealthy obsession with zombies, because I am a neuroscientist, foodie, and runner (and zombies hate fast food).
